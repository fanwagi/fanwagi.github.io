About

Dr. Wu, Fan is an Assistant Professor at Wuhan University, Wuhan, China. His research interests include Search Based Software Engineering, Genetic Improvement, Mutation Testing, Machine Learning, and Evolutionary Algorithms.

Fan received his PhD award at University College London in 2017, supervised by Prof. Mark Harman, Dr. Jens Krinke, and Dr. Yue Jia. His PhD thesis focuses on Genetic Improvement of software using Search Based Software Engineering and Mutation Testing approaches. Fan received his Bachelor degree from Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University in 2011, after which, he worked as a Research Assistant in State Key Laboratory of Intelligent Technology and Systems, Tsinghua University.

Fan also worked as Data Analyst and System Architect at BNP Paribas(London) from 2016 to 2017.

Student Projects

UCL Msc Dissertation Project: Trading Strategy Optimisation

In Quantitative Finance, a good trading strategy is essential to securing profit. Though many strategies are well tested and manually adjusted on historical data, their performance is limited by human knowledge and vulnerable to human errors. In this project, we use Machine Learning approaches to automatically improve a trading strategy, including code optimisation and parameter tuning, so that it can be improved in a way that quant researchers have never thought of before.


UCL Msc Dissertation Project: Indicator Study of Trading Strategies

In Quantitative Finance, whether a trading strategy is good or not usually depends on the performance indicators we use. Different indicators may favor different aspects of a strategy. This project is to study existing strategy indicators and potentially propose a new indicator that balances multiple aspects of a strategy.
